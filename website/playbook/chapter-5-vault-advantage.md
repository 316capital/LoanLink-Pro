## What 100% Financing Actually Means

Let's clarify terminology, because "100% financing" gets thrown around loosely.

**Traditional BRRRR financing:**
- Lender funds 75-80% of total cost (purchase + rehab)
- You bring 20-25% as down payment and reserves
- After refinance, you pull out 75-80% of ARV
- **Result:** You typically leave 5-15% of your capital in each deal

**Vault financing:**
- Lender funds 100% of total cost (purchase + rehab + closing)
- You bring $0 at closing (just reserves for holding costs during construction)
- After refinance, you pull out 75-80% of ARV
- **Result:** You recover 100%+ of your invested capital (sometimes making money on the refinance)

---

### Traditional BRRRR: 75-80% LTC

**Example:**
```
Purchase Price:        $150,000
Rehab Budget:          $50,000
Closing Costs:         $5,000
TOTAL COST:            $205,000

Loan Amount (80% LTC): $164,000
Your Down Payment:     $41,000
```

You need $41,000 to close the deal. After stabilization, you refinance:

```
ARV:                   $280,000
Refinance (75% LTV):   $210,000
Capital Recovered:     $210,000
Your Original Cost:    $41,000 (down payment) + ~$8,000 (holding costs) = $49,000

Cash Left in Deal:     $49,000 - $210,000 = -$0 (you pulled out $161,000, but paid back the original loan of $164,000)
```

Wait, let's recalculate accurately:

```
Refinance Proceeds:    $210,000
Payoff Original Loan:  -$164,000
Net to You:            $46,000
Your Cash Invested:    $41,000 (down) + $8,000 (holding) = $49,000
Cash Left in Deal:     $3,000
```

You recovered 94% of your capital. Not bad—but you still have $3K stuck in the deal.

## Who Qualifies for Vault

Vault isn't for everyone. It's for serious, experienced investors who have proven they can execute.

Here are the requirements:

---

### 1. Credit Score: 700+

We need to see financial stability. A 700+ score shows you manage debt responsibly.

**What if you're at 680-699?**  
You might still qualify if your deal experience is strong (5+ flips in 36 months) and you have significant liquidity.

**What if you're below 680?**  
Focus on building your credit first. Do 2-3 deals with traditional financing, pay everything on time, and reapply once you hit 700.

---

### 2. Experience: 3+ Flips in 36 Months

We need to know you can execute. You understand contractors, timelines, budgets, and exits.

**What counts as a "flip"?**
- Fix & Flip (purchased, rehabbed, sold)
- BRRRR (purchased, rehabbed, rented, refinanced)
- New construction (ground-up build completed and sold/rented)

**What doesn't count:**
- Wholesaling (you never owned the property)
- Turnkey purchases (no rehab/value-add)
- Partial flips (you started but didn't finish)

**Partnerships count.** If you partnered on 5 deals, that's 5 deals. But we want to see that *you* were operationally involved (not just a money partner).

---

### 3. Track Record: Proven Exits

We want to see that your deals actually *work*.

**What we look for:**
- Did you sell at or above your projected price?
- Did your refinances appraise where you expected?
- Did you finish on time (or close to it)?
- Did you stay on budget (or close to it)?

**Red flags:**
- Deals that took 12+ months when projected for 6
- Refinances that appraised 15%+ below expectations
- Projects abandoned or sold at a loss

**How to prove it:**
Submit a deal resume with:
- Addresses
- Purchase prices
- Rehab budgets (estimated vs. actual)
- Sale prices or ARVs
- Timelines (estimated vs. actual)
- Photos (before/after)

---

### 4. Liquidity: 6 Months Reserves

Even with 100% LTC financing, you need to fund holding costs (interest, taxes, insurance, utilities) during the rehab.

**Typical holding costs:** $2,000-4,000/month per property

If you're doing 3-4 deals simultaneously, you need $20-40K in liquid reserves.

**What counts as liquidity:**
- Cash in checking/savings
- Money market accounts
- Lines of credit (unused, available balance)

**What doesn't count:**
- Equity in properties (not liquid)
- Retirement accounts (not accessible without penalty)
- Crypto (too volatile)

**Minimum requirement:** $25,000 liquid reserves at time of Vault application.
